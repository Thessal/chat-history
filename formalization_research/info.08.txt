Q&A on this book
Q1 : Identify causal relation that distingushes Kuramoto model from ACO in the context of synchronized behavior of ant colony / convergence of PSO.
A1 : Interaction in ACO is mediated by time-evolving scalar field ϕ(x,t). However ACO do not require phycial symmetry and conservation laws, resulting different application field. Approximately speaking, PSO is non-physical version of mean-field approximation, resulting distinguished utility of the method over models.
Q2 : Define difference between multiobjective optimization and optimization of linear combinations of objectives. In this context, what difference makes PSO distinguished from the linear optimization?
A2 : PSO can determin pareto front, not a single optimal point.
Q3 : Is it factually true that PSO can generate a probability distribution of the optimal solution candidates? Does it imply that MOPSO can be applied to multiple objectives (for example sharpe and drawdown) of Markowitzian portfolio, and assign weights to the stocks instead of finding the best stock? Identify the root cause of this difference, if the inference is true.
A3 : It creats empirical ensemble. The definition of the solution space causes the difference.
Q4 : While the initial lbest model with ring topology is a good abstraction, I'd like to generalize it to strogatz's ring lattice with rewiring, and barabasi's scale-free. A distinguishing characteristic between these network is correlation length. I guess correlation length is an important parameter for finding optimal and diverse solution.  
A4 : MOPSO requires suppressing the correlation length for the diversity
