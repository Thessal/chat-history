Standard symbolic regression algorithms struggle with complex, generic functions unless they exploit simplifying underlying properties such as symmetry, separability, or units.

Neural networks can be deployed as high-dimensional interpolators to systematically test for and discover translational, rotational, or scaling symmetries within raw data.

Identifying these symmetries allows the primary dataset to be mathematically transformed and broken down into simpler components with fewer independent variables, mitigating overfitting and combinatorial explosion during symbolic regression.

Purely empirical financial machine learning models frequently learn spurious correlations that violate fundamental economic principles, such as the absence of arbitrage, causing them to fail during dynamic regime shifts.

Theoretical market symmetries and rules can be enforced during empirical model training by embedding partial differential equations (e.g., the Feynman-Kac PDE for no-arbitrage conditions) directly into the loss function.

Constrained, physics-informed latent dynamics can be subsequently distilled through a differentiable symbolic bottleneck to output sparse, human-readable mathematical expressions and trading rules.
