# Deep Symbolic Regression: Recovering Mathematical Expressions from Data via Risk-Seeking Policy Gradients
**Authors**: Brenden K. Petersen, Mikel Landajuela Larma, T. Nathan Mundhenk, Claudio P. Santiago, Soo K. Kim, Joanne T. Kim (ICLR 2021)

## General Facts
* Symbolic regression is NP-hard.
* GP scales poorly.
* Constraints in GP require post hoc reversion of evolutionary operation when equation is invalid. This is harmful to diversity, because it restricts search space.
* GraphRNN constructs breadth-first, but depth-first traversal is more natural.
* The pre-order traversal sequence maps identically to unique mathematical trees.
* AutoML is black-box program synthesis.

## Methodology
* Placeholder for constants (skeleton) were generated by the model, and BFGS was applied for the constants.
* Parent and sibling tokens are the inputs of the RNN.
* Sequential search using RNN with policy gradient.
* Constraints were applied by zeroing out the probabilities and normalizing the rest.
* Reward = 1/(1+NRMSE)
* Ablation study on risk, entropy, and constraints.

## Claims
* Risk-seeking policy gradient maximizes best-case compared to expectation-based counterparts.
* Proposed method is robust against noise in regression datapoints.
* DSR outperforms conventional priority queue training baselines on standard benchmarks.

## Novelty
* Combining BFGS and RNN for symbolic regression with in-situ constraints for efficient SR.
* Identification of expectation problem in RL based symbolic regression.
